Based on Figure 1 and Figure 3, there is a noticeable correlation between adult literacy rates and GDP per capita, specifically telling us that higher literacy rate is associated with higher GDP per capita. This finding aligns with other research. According to Dr. Rahman from the University of Chittagong, “taking proper initiative to increase literacy rate of a country will reduce its unemployment rate and increase PGDP resulting development of the country” (Rahman 2011). Additionally, the World Literacy Foundation highlights the importance of education in economic development as “the cost of illiteracy to the global economy is estimated at USD $1.19 trillion” in 2022 (Cree, Kay, and Steward 2022).
